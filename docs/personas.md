# User Personas

NexusOS AI serves a range of executive and operational leaders in enterprise SaaS organizations. Each persona has distinct needs, workflows, and jobs to be done.

---

## 1. Executive Leader

**Roles:** CEO, COO, CRO, CPO

**Context:**
This persona is responsible for overall business health and strategic direction. They consume information from multiple functions and need a clear, synthesized view of what is happening across the organization without spending time assembling that view manually.

**Core Needs:**
- Real-time view of business health across revenue, customer success, product, and engineering
- Revenue and churn risk visibility with enough context to act
- Strategic initiative tracking across functions
- Cross-functional bottleneck detection
- Executive-ready summaries that reduce time spent in status meetings

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Understand what needs attention before it becomes a problem
- Make faster, better-informed strategic decisions
- Align teams around shared priorities
- Reduce surprises in board meetings and quarterly business reviews
- Stay informed without becoming a bottleneck in the information flow

**Pain Today:**
Receives information through recurring reports, escalations, and informal Slack updates. Information is retrospective, often incomplete, and requires manual synthesis to connect signals across functions.

---

## 2. Revenue Leader

**Roles:** VP Sales, CRO, RevOps

**Context:**
Responsible for pipeline performance, forecast accuracy, and revenue execution. Manages a team of account executives and works closely with RevOps to understand where revenue is at risk and where it can be accelerated.

**Core Needs:**
- Pipeline risk visibility with early warning on stalled or at-risk deals
- Forecast accuracy with clear explanation of gap drivers
- Deal execution intelligence based on call activity, buyer engagement, and next steps
- Rep productivity insights to identify coaching opportunities
- Expansion signals from CS and product usage data

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which deals are at risk before the end of quarter
- Understand the root cause of pipeline movement
- Coach reps with specific, evidence-based recommendations
- Improve forecast confidence for leadership and board reviews

**Pain Today:**
Relies on CRM data that is often incomplete or stale, manual pipeline reviews, and call recordings that require time to review. Forecast accuracy is low because signals are scattered.

---

## 3. Customer Success Leader

**Roles:** VP Customer Success, CS Operations, CSM Manager

**Context:**
Responsible for customer retention, renewal performance, and expansion revenue. Manages a team of CSMs and works to identify at-risk accounts early and keep customers engaged and growing.

**Core Needs:**
- Continuous customer health monitoring across usage, sentiment, support, and engagement
- Renewal readiness assessments with clear risk flags
- Churn risk detection before accounts reach a critical state
- QBR preparation support using account history and health signals
- Expansion opportunity identification in existing accounts

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which accounts need intervention before they request a call to cancel
- Prepare CSMs with accurate, complete account context
- Improve renewal rates through proactive, data-driven engagement
- Identify accounts ready for expansion conversations

**Pain Today:**
Customer health is tracked in spreadsheets or basic health scores that do not capture the full signal picture. CSMs spend significant time manually preparing for customer calls and QBRs.

---

## 4. Product Leader

**Roles:** CPO, VP Product, Group PM, Staff PM

**Context:**
Responsible for product strategy, roadmap prioritization, and ensuring the product is meeting customer and market needs. Works with input from customers, CS, support, sales, and engineering.

**Core Needs:**
- Customer feedback intelligence drawn from calls, tickets, NPS, CRM notes, and reviews
- Roadmap signal clustering to identify recurring themes across customer segments
- Product gap detection with severity and frequency context
- PRD generation support to accelerate planning cycles
- Strategic theme tracking aligned to company priorities

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Prioritize the roadmap using real customer signal, not the loudest voice
- Understand which product gaps are affecting the most strategically important customers
- Reduce the time from customer insight to product planning artifact
- Communicate roadmap priorities with supporting evidence

**Pain Today:**
Feedback is fragmented across Gong notes, support tickets, CRM fields, and NPS survey responses. Synthesizing this into prioritization decisions is a manual, time-consuming process with significant risk of bias.

---

## 5. Support Leader

**Roles:** VP Support, Support Operations

**Context:**
Responsible for ticket resolution quality, team efficiency, and ensuring support insights feed back into product and engineering. Manages a team that handles high volume and needs to prioritize effectively.

**Core Needs:**
- Ticket trend intelligence to detect emerging issues before they escalate
- Escalation prediction based on sentiment, customer tier, and issue type
- Product quality insights based on patterns across tickets
- Resolution automation for common, well-understood issues
- Support bottleneck visibility to improve team efficiency

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Detect product quality issues from ticket patterns before engineering hears about them
- Reduce time to resolution through intelligent triage and response suggestions
- Ensure high-value customers receive prioritized attention
- Feed actionable product intelligence to the PM team

**Pain Today:**
Ticket triage is largely manual. Product quality signals buried in tickets rarely make it to the right product teams in a structured way. Escalation often happens reactively.

---

## 6. Engineering Leader

**Roles:** VP Engineering, Engineering Manager

**Context:**
Responsible for engineering delivery, release quality, and team execution. Works across sprints, releases, incidents, and dependencies to maintain predictable delivery and minimize production risk.

**Core Needs:**
- Delivery risk visibility based on sprint progress, PR activity, and open blockers
- Sprint and release health monitoring with early warning signals
- Incident intelligence linked to recent releases and engineering activity
- Dependency tracking across teams to surface handoff risks
- Tech debt visibility and its connection to delivery slowdowns

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which releases are at risk before the planned date
- Identify blockers and dependencies early enough to resolve them
- Connect incident patterns to root causes in engineering activity
- Provide accurate delivery timelines to product and leadership

**Pain Today:**
Delivery risk is often identified too late. Sprint data in Jira is not automatically connected to deployment health, incident data, or cross-team dependency context.

---

## 7. HR and People Leader

**Roles:** Head of People, HR Operations

**Context:**
Responsible for employee operations, onboarding, policy management, and workforce planning. Works across hiring, performance, compliance, and employee experience.

**Core Needs:**
- Employee operations visibility across onboarding, performance cycles, and offboarding
- Onboarding intelligence to ensure new hires reach productivity milestones
- Policy automation for common HR questions, approvals, and workflows
- Sentiment and attrition signals to identify retention risks
- Workforce planning insights to align headcount with business priorities

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Ensure new employees are set up for success and not falling through the cracks
- Detect early signals of employee disengagement or attrition risk
- Reduce time spent on repetitive HR questions and administrative workflows
- Keep leadership informed about people-related risks

**Pain Today:**
People data is spread across HRIS, performance tools, onboarding checklists, and informal Slack conversations. Attrition signals and onboarding gaps are often identified too late to intervene effectively.
