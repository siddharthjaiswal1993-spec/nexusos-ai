# Outcome Model — NexusOS AI

How success is measured: what business outcomes the platform creates, what metrics track those outcomes, and what targets indicate the platform is working.

---

## The Value Hypothesis

NexusOS AI creates value by reducing the time and cognitive load required to maintain operational awareness across an enterprise organisation — and by enabling faster, better-informed decisions as a result.

The hypothesis: if AI agents can surface cross-functional risks and opportunities that would otherwise require hours of manual synthesis, then leadership teams will make better decisions faster, and the time freed from coordination work will be redirected to higher-value activities.

Value is realised in four dimensions:
1. **Decisions made earlier** — risks identified before they become crises
2. **Coordination overhead reduced** — fewer status meetings, fewer cross-functional escalations
3. **Executive time freed** — less time spent compiling context, more time spent on strategy
4. **Organisation-level visibility** — leaders can see what the AI sees, not just their own domain

## Product Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Recommendation acceptance rate | >65% | If the system's recommendations are accepted, they are useful |
| False positive rate per agent | <15% | Too many false positives destroys trust |
| Decision log completeness | >95% | Every approved action should have a corresponding log entry |
| Agent uptime | >99.5% | Operational intelligence has to be continuously available |
| Memory freshness | <24-hour staleness | Decisions made on stale context produce bad recommendations |

---

## AI Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Cross-agent reasoning accuracy | Validated on 50 scenario set | The core differentiator requires verified cross-agent accuracy |
| Context retention quality | >90% relevance score on random sample | Memory layer health requires ongoing measurement |
| Confidence calibration | Actual outcomes match predicted confidence bands | A system that is 80% confident should be right 80% of the time |
| Recommendation specificity | >80% include explicit owner and timeline | Vague recommendations do not drive action |

---

## Operational Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Agent recommendation latency | <2 minutes from signal to recommendation | Real-time intelligence has to be genuinely real-time |
| Approval workflow completion rate | >90% resolved within 24 hours | Stale approvals indicate a product problem, not a user problem |
| System reliability | 99.9% uptime | Enterprise tolerance for downtime is very low |
| Data integration health | >99% successful syncs | Stale data means wrong recommendations |

## Success Scenario

A mid-market B2B SaaS company (300 employees, $30M ARR) runs NexusOS AI for 12 months.

**Outcomes measured:**
- Revenue risk detected earlier: 4 material churn risks identified at 60-day horizon vs. 14-day horizon previously
- Expansion opportunities accelerated: 2 upsell cycles shortened from 90 to 45 days through early signal detection
- Executive reporting time reduced: Weekly cross-functional status prep time reduced from 8 hours to 1.5 hours
- Cross-team coordination meetings reduced: Monthly recurring cross-functional syncs reduced by 30% as AI surface replaces status meetings

**Not measured:** Individual productivity gains, copilot usage rates, or model accuracy in isolation. Success is organisational, not individual.

---

## The Metric That Matters Most

**Recommendation acceptance rate over time.**

If the system's recommendations are accepted at >65% and improving month-over-month, the platform is building trust. If acceptance rate is flat or declining, the agents are not calibrating correctly to the organisation's context.

This metric is the canary. Everything else flows from it.
